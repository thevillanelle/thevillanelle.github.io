Program Vision

Why This Summit Exists

Remote employees face five distinct gaps that their in-office colleagues do not. This summit is a direct, intentional answer to all five.

👁️

Visibility Gap

Remote employees are often out of sight — and unintentionally out of mind when it comes to development opportunities, sponsorship, and advancement.

🚪

Access Gap

Organic access to leaders — hallway conversations, lunch runs, impromptu chats — simply does not exist for at-home employees.

🔗

Connection Gap

Remote employees frequently report feeling disconnected from peers, from leadership, and from the broader culture of their organization.

🏆

Recognition Gap

The contributions of remote employees are harder to see — and therefore harder to celebrate. The AHA Showcase is built to fix this directly.

🌱

Growth Gap

Without intentional investment in development programming, remote employees may feel their growth is self-directed and unsupported.

🧭

Belonging Gap

Remote employees can feel like they work for a company without feeling like they are part of one. Connection is about people. Belonging is about identity — and the summit is designed to make membership in this community feel visible, real, and worth showing up for.

"Every remote employee should feel as seen, as connected, and as invested in as any employee sitting in an Apple office."

Operational Detail

Protecting Hourly Employees

This is one of the most important operational details in the entire program and it cannot be an afterthought.

⚠️ The Reality

Many remote employees are hourly. Any session longer than 30 minutes requires advance scheduling by their manager. If this is not communicated clearly and early, employees will want to attend and simply cannot. That is worse than not offering it at all.

Manager communications must go out before employee sign-ups open — so managers are already prepared when their employees come to them.

"Your team members may sign up for sessions during the Remote Leadership Summit that are longer than 30 minutes. Please work with them in advance to ensure these sessions are built into their schedule. This is an approved engagement event and their time to participate is supported."

Session tagging system — used in every communication, every sign-up page, every calendar invite:

🟢 Drop In
No scheduling required

30 minutes or under. Show up when you can.

🟡 Schedule Required
Confirm with your manager first

Over 30 minutes. Advance notice protects you.

🔵 Sign Up
Limited capacity

First come, first served. Caps enforced at 15.

🟣 Leader Present
A leader is in the room

Peer level. They are participants, not presenters.

🤝 WFO Partnership: Removing the Scheduling Barrier

Checking in with your leader before every session is itself a barrier to participation. The WFO (Workforce Operations) partnership is how we eliminate it entirely — without creating more work for managers.

How it works: Employees sign up for sessions through the summit portal as normal. Behind the scenes, WFO receives admin access to the sign-up system and handles all the scheduling on the backend — building approved summit time directly into each employee's schedule without requiring a separate manager conversation for every session.

Employees should arrive at the summit with their schedule already cleared. The logistics happen behind them, not in front of them.

Pre-allocated session budgets — each employee receives a clear participation allowance upfront, so they can plan their week with confidence:

📚
Growth & Development Panels

Up to 3 sessions — sign up for the topics that matter most to you

🤝
Leader 1:1 Sign-Up Blocks

Up to 1 session — your time, your agenda, your leader

☕

🎯
All-Hands Sessions

All included — Opening, Showcase Live, AI Wrapped, and Virtual Arcade are pre-cleared for everyone

Session budgets are illustrative and should be confirmed with WFO and leadership based on team size, coverage needs, and operational requirements. The principle is fixed: employees should know their allocation before the summit starts, not negotiate for it session by session.

Leader Participation

The Rotating Leader Model

Every organizational leader contributes something. Leaders choose their tier and show up fully for what they commit to. No cleared calendars required. The goal: full leader pool coverage over time.

1

Connect Open Door

Opens Connect availability within the org space during summit days. Minimum lift, maximum access for employees.

2

Panel Participant

Joins one small group panel as a participant — not a facilitator. Just another voice in the room.

3

1:1 Sign-Up Block

Opens a block of dedicated 1:1 time for employee sign-ups. Slots are claimed fast. Employee-led agenda.

4

Panel Host

Hosts or co-hosts a small group growth panel. Guides the conversation, but not the only voice in it.

5

Summit Embedded Leader

Participates across multiple days, embedded in sessions alongside employees. Full presence, genuine investment.

6

Opening or Closing Appearance

Appears in the welcome presentation or closing celebration. Visible, human, and genuine — not scripted.

Connect Integration: Leaders refresh their profiles with real human conversation topics — not just titles. An employee who would never cold-message a director might feel completely comfortable booking when they see the director also loves cooking and has been at Apple for 12 years. The shared interests sort makes this discovery effortless.

AHA Showcase

Celebrate the Work, Not Just the People

Remote employees are often the invisible engine behind some of the most important work in their organizations. The AHA Showcase makes that work visible — to peers, to leadership, and to the entire remote community.

The Showcase Site

This is not a slideshow. It is a dedicated, purpose-built page that lives beyond the summit — styled to match the summit brand including the employee map. Each employee gets a card with their photo, name, role, location dot, and their highlight. Searchable by name, team, or location.

Self-Submissions — Brag About Yourself

Explicitly encouraged and normalized. The language around this matters: "This is not the time to be humble. Tell us what you built, what you solved, what you shipped. You earned it." Employees submit a real photo, their highlight, and an optional visual or quote from a teammate.

Peer Submissions — Brag About Someone Else

Anyone can nominate anyone. Leaders nominate team members. Peers call out peers. Submissions can be attributed or anonymous. Peer nominations appear as a callout on the employee's card.

🎞️ Highlight Reel

A curated visual presentation of project highlights submitted by employees or their managers.

🎙️ Employee Spotlights

Short 3–5 minute presentations by employees about a project or contribution they are proud of.

📣 Leader Callouts

Leaders nominate remote employees or teams for standout contributions, shared live during the session.

🖼️ Showcase Wall

A digital async space where employees post their own highlights throughout the summit — no stage required.

The Closing Experience

The summit closes with an AI-generated, Spotify Wrapped–style celebration of the event itself. Employees are the subject — not the audience.

Throughout the summit, a dedicated Slack channel collects reactions, highlights, memorable quotes, and reflections in real time. At the close of Day 4, an AI agent aggregates all submissions and generates a dynamic visual closing presentation. The leadership response is written the day of — not pre-scripted.

Virtual Offsite

The Arcade Closing Experience

Instead of a formal closing session, the summit ends with everyone playing together. Groups are pre-assigned — mixed employees and leaders — sent into a shared virtual experience. There is no hierarchy in Jackbox.

🎮

Jackbox Games

Quiplash, Drawful, Trivia Murder Party. Hosted in a shared video call. No download required for players. Always generates moments.

🎨

Gartic Phone

Collaborative drawing and guessing. Wildly fun and always produces at least three moments people quote for months.

🌍

GeoGuessr

Team-based geography guessing. Especially resonant for a community that is literally spread across a map.

🔐

Online Escape Room

Collaborative problem solving. Great for cross-functional teams who haven't worked together before.

❓

Custom SDS Trivia

Apple history, fun facts pulled from the 2 Truths and a Lie submissions, SDS-specific questions. Built specifically for this community.

Why this works: Play is the fastest way to build authentic connection. Leaders and employees are genuinely peers in a game — there is no hierarchy. It is surprising. No one expects a work event to end with an arcade. It creates inside jokes and memories that outlast the summit. It is the thing people will tell their friends about.

Internal

🌿 The Miro Debrief — Rose, Thorn, Bud, Act

The planning team and participating leaders convene in a shared Miro board while the summit is still warm. The framework is simple. The output is not.

🌹
Rose

What worked. What landed. What we are proud of and want to protect in Year 2.

🌵
Thorn

What was hard. What didn't land. What we would change without hesitation.

🌱
Bud

What has potential. The ideas that came up during the summit worth developing into something more.

⚡
Act

What we commit to doing differently. Named. Owned. With a DRI and a deadline.

The output of this board becomes the org-facing and leader-facing Summit Wrapped Report — a document that captures what leadership heard, what the data showed, and what is happening next. Not a slide deck. A commitment document.

⚡ The Slack Workflows Powering It All

The summit has a dedicated Slack channel — and it has two purpose-built workflows running inside it throughout all four days. Employees don't need to format anything. They just submit. Everything feeds the Wrapped automatically.

🌟 Highlight Workflow

Employees and leaders submit standout moments in real time — a quote that landed, a connection made, a session that surprised them. Structured input that feeds directly into the Wrapped and the Showcase.

📊 Feedback Workflow

Structured session feedback collected in-channel throughout the event. Feeds event metrics alongside the Wrapped content. No post-event scramble — the data is already there when Day 4 closes.

🔥 The Channel Stays Lit

There is already a Slack channel for this community. And right now, it is not very active. The Remote Leadership Summit is the spark that changes that — but only if the channel is treated as an outcome of the event, not just a tool for it.

→
During the summit: The channel is the live pulse of the event. Highlights, reactions, prompts, energy. Employees who are between sessions can feel the summit happening even when they are not in a room.
→
After the summit: The channel becomes the place you stay connected with people you met. The director you had coffee with. The peer from a different team whose Showcase submission you loved. The follow-up to a conversation that went too fast.
→
Throughout the year: Resources, opportunities, wins, questions. The summit creates relationships — the channel gives them a place to live between summits. By the time Year 2 arrives, the channel is already warm.
The ask: After the summit closes, pin the Wrapped and the Showcase site link at the top of the channel. Post a monthly prompt to keep it moving. Celebrate wins in it year-round. When the next Save the Date drops, the channel is already full of people who remember why they care.
